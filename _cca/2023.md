<h1>Singpaore Youth Festival Art Exhibition 2023</h1>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Achievements/2023%20Term%204%20CCA/SYF_2023.jpg">
</div>

<p>The Singapore Youth Festival Art Exhibition celebrates the artist in every
child. It showcases students’ creative ideas, personal aspirations and
hard work put into artistic expressions.</p>
<p>Our Category C pupils’ work, <strong>Looking Towards the Future</strong>,
was selected to be part of the on-site exhibition. These pupils have also
received the Certificate of Recognition for their thoughtful and skillful
work.</p>

<h1>Chinese Street Dance Competition</h1>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Achievements/Academics/2023%20Term%203/chinese%20street%20dance.jpg">
</div>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Achievements/Academics/2023%20Term%203/street%20dance%20competition.jpeg">
</div>
<p>Chinese Street Dance Competition, jointly organised by the Committee to
Promote Chinese Language Learning (CPCLL) and Maxkios aims to use music
and dance to spur pupils to be interested in learning Chinese Language.
A group of our talented Primary 4 and 5 pupils formed a team to participate
in the event. Although they were only&nbsp;awarded&nbsp;Certificate of
Participation, their hard work and efforts were remarkable! We hope more
pupils will participate to experience the beauty of Chinese Language and
love the language!</p>
<p>We thank <strong>Mdm Zhao Yijin</strong> and <strong>Mrs Charmian New</strong> for
choreographing the dance moves and training the pupils.</p>

<h1>CCA Certificate of Accomplishment</h1>
<p>Every year in term 3, we recognise the hard work and commitment of our
P6 pupils in various CCA groups as they step down from their CCA duties.
The past 3 years have been challenging for our current P6 cohort.</p>
<p>For Sports CCA groups, the National School Games were suspended in 2021
and part of 2022 and pupils in our performing arts CCAs were not able to
participate in the highly anticipated SYF competition in 2020.</p>
<p>On top of that, much of the CCA sessions from 2021 and to early 2022 were
held virtually. Nevertheless, our P6 pupils took these changes in their
stride, persevered and attended their CCA sessions both physically and
virtually.</p>
