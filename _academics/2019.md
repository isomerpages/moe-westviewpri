Reading Superstar Competition 2019
----------------------------------

This year, our school has been working with the Chinese Language Unit (Primary) of MOE’s Curriculum Planning and Development Division and experimenting with new approaches to the Chinese language reading programme. Pupils shared their love for reading through designing an extravaganza of book covers, project displays and book reviews. Their work were sent in to participate in the annual Reading Superstar Competition.

  

We are pleased to share that one of our pupils Ker Zhi Yang (6 Empathy) has been awarded the Merit Award (Upper Primary Category). We hope with this recognition, the programme can continue to bring our pupils’ interest in reading to the next level, encouraging them to cultivate reading as a habit.

  

Congratulations to Zhi Yang for a job well done!

iSpeak competition&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;
----------------------

<img src="/images/Capture.jpeg" style="width:240px;height:160px;margin-left:15px;" align="right">

Two of our pupils, Sun Yuqi from 3 Confidence and Ker Zhi Yang from 6 Empathy took part in the I&nbsp;_Speak_&nbsp;competition in April. This competition, organised by the Inlingua Language School, required participants to present a video recording of themselves speaking on the topic:&nbsp;_Why my school is the best._&nbsp;Judging for this competition was based on the following criteria: pronunciation, language skill, vocal variety, body language, relevance and persuasiveness.

  

West View is very proud of Sun Yuqi who finished third in the Junior Category. She received a certificate and a bronze medal. Heartiest congratulations to Yuqi!

Scholastic Read and Write Challenge 2019
----------------------------------------

<img src="/images/abcde.jpeg" style="width:183px;height:240px;margin-left:15px;" align="left">

The Scholastic Read and Write Challenge 2019 is a national-wide contest that requires participants to write an original story using the theme of the sample text for their respective Primary Level. Three winners would be selected from each level.&nbsp;

  

Jonel Dalton from 6 Empathy won a prize for her impressive science fiction themed story about an escape from an asteroid.&nbsp;

  

Jonel stated, “I was thrilled to win as I had not won the previous year despite taking part.”&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;

Rakan Bahasa
------------

As part of Bulan Bahasa (Malay Language Month), the&nbsp;**Rakan Bahasa**&nbsp;initiative is organised by the Malay Language Council Singapore (Majlis Bahasa) to encourage students who are passionate about the Malay Language and culture, to spread the love of the language to their peers, friends and families.

  

Bulan Bahasa (Malay Language Month) was first launched in 1988 by the Malay Language Council Singapore, as an initiative to encourage the Malay community to embrace the Malay language in their daily lives. Users of the Malay language are heirs of the rich Malay language, and can ensure the continuity of the language through continuous usage.

  

From being a biennial event, Bulan Bahasa, since 2011, has become an annual event. The various activities of Bulan Bahasa have been created to promote and instil greater confidence in and mastery of the Malay language.

  

We are proud to announce our 2 pupils from West View Primary, Nur Sabrina Binte Shahmudin (5E) and Muhammad Haritz Dzikri Bin Herman (5E) were both appointed as&nbsp;**Rakan Bahasa**&nbsp;on Saturday, 13 April 2019.

  

They will be given a set of tasks which revolve around skills such as reading, writing, speaking and other activities that support Malay Language learning.

  

The teacher-in-charge is Mdm Suriani Abd Bakri will assist by training them to be the spokesperson for events as well as prepare them in the upcoming Newsmaker competition.

OPS BACA
--------

The Zhenghua Malay Activity Executive Committee (MAEC) organised OPS BACA on 16 March 2019, Saturday from 9.00am to 1.30pm at Zhenghua Community Club. Mr Liang Eng Hwa, MP for Holland Bukit Timah GRC graced this event as the Guest-of-Honour. Our principal, Mr Eddie Foo was present throughout the event to give his utmost support to our pupils and teachers.

  

OPS BACA aims to heighten awareness and excite pupils on reading habits as well as provide opportunities for them to showcase their talents. On top of that, it is hope that through reading, pupils will know more about classic Malay stories and build confidence through snippets of their acts and recital of poetries. Through this competition, we hope this would encourage pupils to be creative and have fun learning the Malay Language.

  

West View Primary clinched 1st&nbsp;positions for both Primary 3 &amp; Primary 6 categories and 2nd&nbsp;for Primary 4. Due to their winnings, our school emerged as the Overall Champion School for Ops Baca 2019. We would like to congratulate the Malay Language teachers who have worked hard to train the pupils for this special event. Our teachers told the pupils to put in their best and enjoy themselves on stage.
