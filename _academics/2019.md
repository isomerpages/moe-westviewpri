Reading Superstar Competition 2019
----------------------------------

This year, our school has been working with the Chinese Language Unit (Primary) of MOE’s Curriculum Planning and Development Division and experimenting with new approaches to the Chinese language reading programme. Pupils shared their love for reading through designing an extravaganza of book covers, project displays and book reviews. Their work were sent in to participate in the annual Reading Superstar Competition.

  

We are pleased to share that one of our pupils Ker Zhi Yang (6 Empathy) has been awarded the Merit Award (Upper Primary Category). We hope with this recognition, the programme can continue to bring our pupils’ interest in reading to the next level, encouraging them to cultivate reading as a habit.

  

Congratulations to Zhi Yang for a job well done!

iSpeak competition    
----------------------

<img src="/images/Capture.jpeg" style="width:240px;height:160px;margin-left:15px;" align = "right">

Two of our pupils, Sun Yuqi from 3 Confidence and Ker Zhi Yang from 6 Empathy took part in the I _Speak_ competition in April. This competition, organised by the Inlingua Language School, required participants to present a video recording of themselves speaking on the topic: _Why my school is the best._ Judging for this competition was based on the following criteria: pronunciation, language skill, vocal variety, body language, relevance and persuasiveness.

  

West View is very proud of Sun Yuqi who finished third in the Junior Category. She received a certificate and a bronze medal. Heartiest congratulations to Yuqi!

Scholastic Read and Write Challenge 2019
----------------------------------------

<img src="/images/abcde.jpeg" style="width:183px;height:240px;margin-left:15px;" align = "left">

The Scholastic Read and Write Challenge 2019 is a national-wide contest that requires participants to write an original story using the theme of the sample text for their respective Primary Level. Three winners would be selected from each level. 

  

Jonel Dalton from 6 Empathy won a prize for her impressive science fiction themed story about an escape from an asteroid. 

  

Jonel stated, “I was thrilled to win as I had not won the previous year despite taking part.”            

  

For her efforts, Jonel won $50 worth of books and received a certificate. Congratulations, Jonel!
